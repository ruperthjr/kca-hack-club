# Team Geek: A Software Developer's Guide to Working Well with Others

**Authors:** Brian W. Fitzpatrick, Ben Collins-Sussman  
**Focus:** Building effective software development teams

## Overview

Programming is a team sport. Learn how to collaborate effectively, build trust, handle difficult people, and create a positive team culture that ships great software.

## The Three Pillars: HRT

### Humility
> "You are not the center of the universe. You're neither omniscient nor infallible. You're open to self-improvement."

- Acknowledge mistakes
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Share credit
- Learn from others

### Respect
> "You genuinely care about others you work with. You treat them kindly and appreciate their abilities and accomplishments."

- Value teammates' opinions
- Listen actively
- Give constructive feedback
- Assume good intentions

### Trust
> "You believe others are competent and will do the right thing, and you're OK with letting them drive when appropriate."

- Delegate effectively
- Share responsibility
- Be reliable
- Admit when you don't know

## Building Team Culture

### Create Psychological Safety

**Safe to:**
- Ask questions

**How:**

- Celebrate failures as learning
- Encourage experimentation
- No blame culture

**Asynchronous:**

**Choose wisely based on:**

## Dealing with Difficult People

### The Poisonous Person

**Warning Signs:**
- Insults and meanness
- Intolerance
- Selfishness
- Lack of respect
- Undermining team

**Strategies:**
1. Address directly
2. Document behavior
3. Set boundaries
4. Involve management if needed
5. Sometimes: remove from team

### The Perfectionist

**Characteristics:**
- Never satisfied
- Blocks progress
- Analysis paralysis
- Criticism without action

**Handle by:**
- Set clear deadlines
- Define "good enough"
- Time-box discussions
- Focus on value

- Small, focused changes
- Clear descriptions
- Self-review first
- Be open to feedback

**For Reviewers:**
- Be kind and specific

## Handling Conflict

### Healthy Disagreement

**Steps:**
1. Understand the issue
2. Listen to all viewpoints
3. Focus on facts, not people
4. Find common ground
5. Propose solutions
6. Agree on path forward

### Escalation

**When to escalate:**
- Repeated pattern
- No resolution
- Team impact
- Blocking progress

**How to escalate:**
- Document the issue
- Attempted solutions
- Impact on work
- Specific examples
