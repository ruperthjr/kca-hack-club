## Communication Templates

### Professional Email Template
```
Subject: [Action Required/FYI/Request]: [Specific Topic]

Hi [Name],

[Purpose statement in one sentence]

[Context/Background - 2-3 sentences]

[Main message - bullet points if multiple items]
- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

[Call to action or next steps]

[Closing - appropriate to relationship]
[Signature]
```

### Meeting Agenda Template
```
Meeting: [Title]
Date: [Date]
Time: [Time]
Location: [Location/Link]
Attendees: [Names]

Objectives:
1. [Objective 1]
2. [Objective 2]

Agenda:
1. [Item 1] - [Time allocation] - [Owner]
2. [Item 2] - [Time allocation] - [Owner]

Materials:
- [Document 1]
- [Document 2]

Action Items:
[Will be filled during meeting]
```

### Presentation Structure
```
1. Opening (10%)
   - Hook
   - Topic introduction
   - Credibility
   - Preview

2. Body (75%)
   - Main Point 1 (with evidence)
   - Main Point 2 (with evidence)
   - Main Point 3 (with evidence)

3. Conclusion (15%)
   - Summary
   - Call to action
   - Memorable closing
```

## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### Mistake 1: Too Much Jargon
**Problem**: Confuses audience, excludes people  
**Solution**: Know your audience, define terms

### Mistake 2: Burying the Lead
**Problem**: Main point lost in details  
**Solution**: Start with the most important information

### Mistake 3: Poor Email Subject Lines
**Problem**: Emails ignored or missed  
**Solution**: Be specific, include action if needed

### Mistake 4: Death by PowerPoint
**Problem**: Slides are unreadable, too much text  
**Solution**: One idea per slide, large font, visuals

### Mistake 5: Not Listening
**Problem**: Miscommunication, conflict  
**Solution**: Practice active listening, ask questions

### Mistake 6: Unclear Expectations
**Problem**: Tasks not completed correctly  
**Solution**: Be specific, confirm understanding

### Mistake 7: Wrong Communication Channel
**Problem**: Message ineffective  
**Solution**: Choose appropriate medium

### Mistake 8: Passive Voice Overuse
**Problem**: Unclear responsibility, weak writing  
**Solution**: Use active voice when possible

## Public Speaking Tips

### Preparation
1. Know your material cold
2. Practice out loud (minimum 5 times)
3. Time yourself
4. Prepare for questions
5. Visit venue if possible

### Delivery
1. Start strong (first 30 seconds critical)
2. Make eye contact with individuals
3. Vary your pace and volume
4. Use purposeful gestures
5. Embrace pauses
6. Show enthusiasm
7. Connect with stories

### Managing Nervousness
1. Deep breathing exercises
2. Arrive early
3. Focus on message, not yourself
4. Remember audience wants you to succeed
5. Start with something easy
6. Practice power poses

## Business Etiquette

### Email Etiquette
- Respond within 24 hours
- Use professional email address
- Keep attachments under 10MB
- Reply vs. Reply All (choose wisely)
- Include clear subject lines
- Proofread before sending

### Meeting Etiquette
- Arrive on time (early for important meetings)
- Come prepared
- Put phone away
- Don't interrupt
- Contribute meaningfully
- Follow up with action items

### Phone Etiquette
- Answer within 3 rings
- Identify yourself clearly
- Listen actively
- Take notes
- Return calls promptly
- Keep conversations focused

### Virtual Meeting Etiquette
- Test technology beforehand
- Appropriate background
- Professional appearance
- Mute when not speaking
- Use video when possible
- Don't multitask

## Networking Skills

### Elevator Pitch Structure
```
1. Who you are (5 seconds)
2. What you do (10 seconds)
3. What makes you unique (10 seconds)
4. What you're looking for (5 seconds)
```

**Example**:
"Hi, I'm Maryphin, a computer science student at KCA University specializing in data analytics. I recently built a machine learning model that predicts student performance with 85% accuracy. I'm passionate about using data to solve educational challenges, and I'm looking for internship opportunities in ed-tech companies. What do you do?"

### Networking Conversation Tips
1. Ask open-ended questions
2. Listen more than you talk (60/40 rule)
3. Find common ground
4. Be genuinely interested
5. Exchange contact information
6. Follow up within 48 hours

## Active Listening Techniques

### The RASA Method
**R**eceive: Pay attention  
**A**ppreciate: Make small acknowledgments (hmm, yes, okay)  
**S**ummarize: "So what you're saying is..."  
**A**sk: Ask questions to clarify

### Levels of Listening
1. **Ignoring**: Not paying attention
2. **Pretending**: Appear to listen
3. **Selective**: Hear only what you want
4. **Attentive**: Focus on words
5. **Empathetic**: Understand feelings and intent

**Aim for Level 5**

## Feedback Guidelines

### Giving Feedback (SBI Model)
**S**ituation: Describe when and where  
**B**ehavior: Describe what you observed  
**I**mpact: Explain the effect

**Example**:
"During yesterday's presentation (Situation), I noticed you were reading directly from your slides (Behavior), which made it harder for the audience to engage with your ideas (Impact). Try maintaining more eye contact and using the slides as prompts rather than a script."

### Receiving Feedback
1. Listen without defending
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Thank the person
4. Reflect on the feedback
5. Act on valid points

## Cross-Cultural Communication Tips

### High-Context vs. Low-Context Cultures

**High-Context (Japan, Arab countries)**:
- Indirect communication
- Reading between the lines
- Relationship-focused
- Non-verbal cues important

**Low-Context (US, Germany, Switzerland)**:
- Direct communication
- Explicit messages
- Task-focused
- Words matter most

### Communication Styles
- **Direct vs. Indirect**: Be aware of cultural preferences
- **Formal vs. Informal**: Match the culture
- **Silence**: Comfortable in some cultures, awkward in others
- **Eye Contact**: Respectful in some cultures, rude in others
- **Time**: Punctuality expectations vary
