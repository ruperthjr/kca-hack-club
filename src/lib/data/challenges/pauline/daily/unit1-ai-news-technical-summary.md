# Technical Summary of an AI News Article

## Overview

Effectively translating recent AI advancements into clear, technical summaries is a vital skill for computer science professionals. This challenge tasks you with selecting a recent, reputable AI news article and producing a concise technical summary that communicates the core innovation, methodology, results, and significance to a technical audience.

## Objective

Summarize a recent AI news article in approximately 250 words, capturing the problem addressed, the technical approach, key results, and broader impact, with proper citation.

## Prerequisites

- Ability to navigate and evaluate reputable AI news sources.
- Understanding of basic AI terminology (e.g., model, dataset, benchmark).
- Familiarity with citation formats (APA or MLA).
- Proficiency in paraphrasing and technical writing.

## Instructions

### Part 1: Select a News Article

- Choose an AI-related article published within the last six months from a reputable source (see Resources).
- Ensure the article discusses a technical innovation, not just a product announcement.

### Part 2: Extract Key Technical Points

- Identify the problem addressed by the AI system.
- Summarize the method, including models, datasets, or techniques used.
- Note the results or performance achieved.
- Explain the significance or potential impact.

### Part 3: Write the Technical Summary

- Begin with the organization and main achievement.
- Briefly describe the problem and context.
- Summarize the technical approach (2–3 sentences).
- Highlight results and implications.
- Conclude with future directions or broader impact.
- Include a full citation in APA or MLA format.

## Deliverables

1. A 250-word technical summary of the chosen article.
2. Proper citation of the original article (APA or MLA).
3. Optional: 100-word executive summary, comparison with original research, or relevant diagram.

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria            | Weight | Description                                              |
|---------------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Source quality      | 10%    | Article is recent, reputable, and technically substantive|
| Technical accuracy  | 30%    | Details are correct and not oversimplified               |
| Clarity & conciseness| 30%   | Summary is well-structured and within word limit         |
| Citation            | 10%    | Citation is complete and properly formatted              |
| Writing mechanics   | 20%    | Grammar, spelling, and professional tone                 |

## Tips & Common Mistakes

- Paraphrase in your own words; do not copy sentences.
- Define acronyms and technical terms on first use.
- Stay within the word limit (250–300 words).
- Use the article’s references for technical accuracy.

## Bonus Challenges (Optional)

1. Compare the news article with the original research paper.
2. Write a 100-word executive summary.
3. Include a relevant diagram or figure with attribution.

## Submission

Submit your summary as a PDF or Word document named `AISummary_YourName.pdf`. Optionally, include a link to the original article.
