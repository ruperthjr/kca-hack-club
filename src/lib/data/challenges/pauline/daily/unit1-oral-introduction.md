# Oral Introduction Challenge: Team Meeting Presentation

## Overview

A compelling self-introduction is essential for establishing credibility and building rapport in professional settings. This challenge guides you through preparing and delivering a 2-minute introduction for a team meeting, focusing on structure, delivery, and adaptability for various contexts.

## Objective

By completing this challenge, you will create and present a structured, confident, and engaging oral introduction, demonstrating mastery of professional communication techniques.

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of professional communication
- Access to a recording device and quiet space
- Familiarity with video playback tools

## Instructions

### Part 1: Structure Your Introduction

Organize your introduction into five sections:
- **Opening:** Greeting, name, role, and positive statement.
- **Professional Background:** Education, career highlights, and transition.
- **Skills & Expertise:** Key technical/soft skills, achievements, certifications.
- **Current Role & Contribution:** Responsibilities, projects, goals.
- **Closing & Connection:** Enthusiasm, invitation to connect, thanks.

Draft your script using these sections and keep each concise.

### Part 2: Practice and Record

- Rehearse your script out loud, focusing on pacing (140-160 wpm), clarity, and non-verbal cues.
- Practice in front of a mirror and record yourself.
- Review your recording, refine delivery, and ensure all sections are included.

### Part 3: Self-Evaluate and Reflect

- Use the provided self-assessment checklist to evaluate content, delivery, and professionalism.
- Reflect on strengths and areas for improvement.
- Revise your script or delivery as needed.

## Deliverables

1. 2-minute video introduction (MP4 or MOV)
2. Final script or bullet-point outline
3. Completed self-assessment checklist
4. Reflection on strengths and improvements

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria         | Weight | Description                                      |
|------------------|--------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Content          | 40%    | Structure, relevance, clarity, and completeness  |
| Delivery         | 40%    | Vocal quality, pacing, articulation, non-verbal  |
| Professionalism  | 20%    | Appearance, confidence, engagement, authenticity |

## Tips & Common Mistakes

- Practice multiple times and time yourself
- Avoid filler words; use pauses for emphasis
- Maintain eye contact and confident posture
- Check audio and video quality before submitting
- Don’t rush—focus on clarity and connection

## Bonus Challenges (Optional)

1. Incorporate a brief, relevant personal story
2. Adapt your introduction for a virtual or international audience
